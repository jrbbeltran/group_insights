Notable Leadership Positions & Organizational Impact

Academy of Management & Professional Association Leadership

  • Presidents & Division Chairs: Multiple scholars have served as AOM Division Chairs (DEI, OB, HR), President of Eastern Academy of Management, President of IberoAmerican Academy of Management, and Co-President of LaFamilia Management Scholars.
  • Executive & Governance Roles: AOM Executive Committee members, PDW Chairs, Program Chairs, and long-term leadership in the PhD Project and Management Faculty of Color Association.
  • Foundational Roles: Founding Editor-in-Chief of The Business Journal of Hispanic Research and key architects of national DEI initiatives in management education.

Institutional & Administrative Leadership

  • Deans & Provosts: Current and former Deans of major business schools (e.g., Rady School at UC San Diego, Bentley University) and Vice Presidents of Academic Affairs / Provosts.
  • Department Chairs & Directors: Multiple Department Chairs, Directors of International MBA programs, Directors of Online Graduate Programs, and coordinators of core undergraduate courses.
  • Curriculum & Program Innovation: Leaders in launching new degrees (e.g., Master’s in Leadership, Leadership Certificates), AI integration initiatives, and large-scale interdisciplinary course redesigns serving thousands of students.

Other Notable Insights

Editorial Influence

Strong presence on editorial boards and as Associate Editors (Journal of Managerial Psychology, Personnel Review, Entrepreneurship Theory and Practice, etc.). Several scholars have founded or led journals focused on Hispanic/Latinx scholarship.

Global & Cross-Cultural Reach

Visiting professorships and research collaborations across Latin America, Europe, Asia, and Australia. Significant work on cross-cultural leadership, Latin American business contexts, and international new ventures.

Mentorship Pipeline

Exceptional track record of developing the next generation — many former PhD students now hold tenure-track positions at top research universities and continue the group’s focus on rigorous, inclusive, and impactful scholarship.
